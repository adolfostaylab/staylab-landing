01 · The old lens

The industry thinks in occupancy.

Decades of hospitality software optimized a single number — how many rooms are full tonight. Meanwhile, everything else a hotel owns sits outside the revenue conversation entirely.

  • Unused inventory

    Rooftops, meeting rooms, parking, storage — square meters that earn nothing between bookings.

  • Premium upgrades

    Better rooms are given away at check-in as favors instead of priced and sold as products.

  • Ancillary services

    Spa, dining, experiences — run as amenities to justify the rate, not as revenue lines of their own.

  • FF&E lifecycle

    Furniture and equipment are bought at retail, depreciated in the dark, and thrown away at a loss.

  • Disconnected revenue

    What little asset revenue exists lives in spreadsheets and side deals — unmeasured, so never optimized.
